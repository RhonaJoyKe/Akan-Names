About

The Akan are the largest ethnic group in both Ghana and the Ivory Coast.
The Akan speak Kwa languages which are part of the larger Niger-Congo family.
From the 15th century to the 19th century, the Akan people dominated gold mining and the gold trade in the region.

Akan names are derived from Ghanian culture.
Frequently in Ghana, children are given their first name as a 'day name' which corresponds to the day in the week they were born.
